Alcohol expenditure vs. Deaths related with Alcohol

Study based on UK National Health Service (NHS) database

Sergio Astorga
Data Scientist

Objectives

The aim of this study is to show a relation between the consumption of alcohol and the mortality raise in the UK.

To that end, a Shiny App has been developed and it is live in this link:

https://serastpan.shinyapps.io/AlcoholUKApp/

The data, provided by the National Health Service (NHS) in the UK, can be retrieved in the following webpage:

https://data.gov.uk/data/search?theme-primary=Health

The data has records from 2001 to 2014 for Expenditure and Deaths year by year and totals across the whole country.

Shiny App (Control)

The App is mainly made up for selecting several variables related with the Expenditures on Alcohol or the Deaths and the causes related with Alcohol that produced those deaths.

Furthermore, a slider has been set for the user to focus on a single year.

Depending on the tab the user is, the actuation with the control will provide a different outcome.

Shiny App (Tabs)

Selecting the different tabs above the images displayed at the App, the user is able to:

  1. Compare the two variables
  2. Compare one variable in subsequent years
  3. Look at the whole picture (Total Deaths vs. Total Alcohol consumption)

  • Totals Summary -> Look interactively at Total number of Deaths per Year vs. Total Expenditure per Year
  • Death vs. Spend exploration -> Compare Death vs. Expenditure trends across the Years or centered in one Year
  • Death split per Year -> Focus on the more relevant illnesses per Year
  • Historical spent on Alcohol -> Explore the evolution of the spendings througout the Years and the variation according to the previous Year
